The Partner Approach

How the conversation goes.
In real life.

Murkemz doesn't cold-call. He walks in as the face of PHX. The pitch is a handshake, not a sales deck.

1

"We're building Phoenix's culture platform."

Open with the vision, not the price. Murkemz has 2.1M monthly IG views. The introduction matters before the offer.

2

"We're selecting 50 founding partners who represent the city."

This is a curated list, not a directory. You're inviting them to be part of a movement, not a listing service.

3

"Free for 90 days. Zero risk."

Remove the friction. They have nothing to lose, and you get their audience as organic distribution.

4

"After 90 days, the data decides."

Show them real numbers: X members visited, Y redeemed the perk, Z were first-time customers. The ROI sells itself.

5

"Founding Partner badge stays forever."

Status matters in culture. Being a founding partner means you were part of the city's platform before it was big.

"I'm not selling you an ad. I'm asking you to be part of the city's culture platform. PHX is blowing up — the song, the app, the whole movement. You can be a founding partner and tell your customers you helped build this. Or you can join later when everyone else already is."
— The Murkemz Pitch

Every business in Phoenix wants to reach young, culturally connected consumers. PHX Pass members are exactly that. Businesses aren't buying software — they're buying audience access to a community that trusts the platform.
